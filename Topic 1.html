Freedom 7

On the 5th of May 1961 at 14:34 UTC a Redstone MRLV MR-7 rocket lifted off from a launch pad at Cape Canaveral LC-5. This was the first flight of the human part of the human flight program. abord was astronaut john glen who was the first American to fly above the Kármán line.

The flight

It is estimated 45 million people watched the launch live as john glen lifted off the launch pad, After lift-off, the rocket accelerated slowly off the launch pad. As the rocket burned its flue it got lighter and the acceleration started to climb, at its max point, the spacecraft was pulling 6.3g. The max speed of the spacecraft was 8,340 km/h at a max altitude of 187.5 km, far above the Kármán line. After reaching apogee john glen took control of the spacecraft and flew it through re-entry until the automatic system took over. The spacecraft pulled 11.6g during re-entry. The parachute was deployed at 6.4km slowing the spacecraft down until is spaced down in the ocean in good condition.

Mission objectives

The main mission objects were to test critical systems of the spacecraft. The first objective was to test the RCS (reaction control system) while in space. This went well as the tasks were conducted successfully. The second task was to look out the periscope of the spacecraft and record what he saw, he noted continents, lakes and rivers however noting that it was hard to identify cities. However, the last task was to change the filter on the periscope, this task was unsuccessful as his hand didn’t fit as it got very close to the launch escape system lever.
